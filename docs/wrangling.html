2.1 Intro, Data vis

This week will provide an introduction to data science using R. You will be introduced to data science through the lens of exploratory data analysis, beginning with the Explore section of the R4DS textbook, read and work through the following chapters:

2.1.1 Reading this week

  • R4DS Chapter 2 Introduction

  • R4DS Chapter 4 Dta vis

  • R4DS Chapter 3 Workflow basics

  • palmerpenguins package homework

2.2 Data workflows

This week will cover the surprisingly important topic of data workflows including input and output. In the Wrangle section of the textbook, read and work through the following chapters:

2.2.1 Reading this week

  • R4DS Chapter 6 Workflow scripts

  • R4DS Chapter 8 Workflow projects

  • Wickham. 2014. Tidy Data. Journal of Statistical Software, 59(10), 1–23. DOI: 10.18637/jss.v059.i10

  • here package homework

  • Jenny Bryan’s view on project-oriented workflows

2.3 Data transformations and wrangling

This week will cover changing, creating, reordering variables in order to wrangle and organise data. Ideas of wider data vs longer data (and messy) are important here too.

2.3.1 Reading this week

  • R4DS Chapter 5 Data transformation

  • R4DS Chapter 9, 10, 11, 12

  • Karl W. Broman & Kara H. Woo (2018) Data Organization in Spreadsheets, The American Statistician, 72:1, 2-10, DOI: 10.1080/00031305.2017.1375989

2.4 Exploration, data types

This will continue to cover more wrangling and dealing with varying data types.

2.4.1 Reading this week

  • R4DS Chapter 9, 10, 11, 12 continued

  • Amelia McNamara & Nicholas J. Horton (2018) Wrangling Categorical Data in R, The American Statistician, 72:1, 97-104, DOI: 10.1080/00031305.2017.1356375

  • WHO example in R4DS Chapter 12

2.5 Tidying data, data forms and formats

This week will move from wrangling data to using and creating relational data, dealing with and using strings and rexexps, and ideas around categorical (aka factors) variables.

2.5.1 Reading this week

  • R4DS Chapter 13 Relational data

  • R4DS Chapter 14 Strings

  • R4DS Chapter 15 Factors

  • Amelia McNamara & Nicholas J. Horton (2018) Wrangling Categorical Data in R, The American Statistician, 72:1, 97-104, DOI: 10.1080/00031305.2017.1356375

2.6 Dates, times, time series

This week will cover everyone’s favourites dates and times. Time series data are a special type of data where we may have to consider the temporal autocorrelation as well as the common push to produce forecasts and fables.

2.6.1 Reading this week

  • R4DS Chapter 15 Factors

  • R4DS Chapter 16 Dates and times
